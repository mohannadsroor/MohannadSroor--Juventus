The First Robot

The first physical robot is generally considered to be the Unimate, developed in the 1950s by George Devol and Joseph
F. Engelberger. The Unimate was a robot arm that was used in manufacturing to perform tasks such as
welding and die casting. It was first installed at a General Motors plant in 1961, and its success led to the widespread use of robots in manufacturing.

The Unimate was not the first mechanical device to be called a robot, however. There were earlier examples of devices
that could perform tasks automatically, such as the "turk," a chess-playing machine built in the late
18th century. The turk was actually a hoax, as it was operated by a human hidden inside the device.

Overall, the history of robots is a long and varied one, with the concept of robots being present in myth and legend for centuries, and the development of practical robots beginning in the 20th century.
